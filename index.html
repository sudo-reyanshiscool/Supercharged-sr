02 · The Beginnings

it started in
a school workshop.

April 2024. One workbench. A printable rulebook. A vow that grew into a team, a charter and a car called Dasher.

The first prototype was sketched in Fusion 360 on a Saturday afternoon in April 2024 — barely fitting inside the regulation block, far too heavy, and held together by hopes more than tolerances.

Then we built another. Then a third. We added a CFD pipeline, a CNC partner, an SLS print bureau, and a Python race model that grew past nine hundred lines. We won regionals. We took silver at nationals. We talked our way into a flight to Singapore.

By the time Dasher rolled into Resorts World Sentosa on 25 September 2025, we'd planted a tree for every prototype, mentored other teams at our own school's regionals, and stood on stage with the F4 paddock at Coimbatore.

It started in a school workshop. It became an Indian flag at the Aramco STEM Racing World Finals.

04 · The Innovations

four ideas
that earn their grams.

Every gram of mass needs to justify itself in milliseconds. These four innovations did, and they're why Dasher cleared 1.330 s on the Denford track before Singapore.

Innov · 01

Magnetic
Nosecone

Five-by-two-millimetre notches in body and nose, joined by an N52 neodymium magnet. Snap-swap the nose during testing. No superglue. No aero compromise.

Snap-swap · Field-serviceable
Innov · 02

Split Bearing
System

Dual bearings per wheel hold the axle on a true straight line. Less wobble, less lateral deviation, more of the CO₂ thrust converting into actual forward motion.

Drag share · 21.4 % saved
Innov · 03

Aerofins
+ Halo Lip

Small fins above the halo, a vertical lip below. Dirty incoming air becomes straight streamlines that get pushed cleanly over and around the cockpit.

Inspired · Modern F1
Innov · 04

Mathematical
Race Model

900+ lines of Python integrating thrust curves, wheel friction, drag, tether yaw and guide-base height. Predicts race time, side displacement and yaw before a chip is cut.

Sims · 3,000 Monte Carlo runs
