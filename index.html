01
Doctrine · Vision & Mission

Five students,
one Indian flag,
a world-finals car.

SUPERCHARGED is five engineers, designers and storytellers from The British School, New Delhi. Across eighteen months we designed in Fusion 360, simulated in Ansys, machined in CNC, validated on the Denford track — and arrived in Singapore as Team T62, India.

Our charter is one line long: be the first Indian team to win the Aramco STEM Racing World Finals. Everything we do — every gram, every micron, every millisecond — is measured against that.

Vision

To create a name in STEM Racing while making a positive impact beyond the track — and to lead with innovation, teamwork and sustainability.

Mission

To deliver excellence through defined roles, strong communication and teamwork. To work as a united team embracing every challenge with passion and purpose.

Method

CFD-driven aero. Hybrid CNC + SLS manufacture. Double-bearing wheel system. A Python race model with 900+ lines that predicts our race time before we cut a chip.

Innov · 01

Magnetic Nosecone

5 × 2 mm notches in body and nose, joined by an N52 neodymium magnet. Snap-swap during testing without superglue, without compromising aero alignment.

Snap-swap · Field-serviceable
Innov · 02

Split Bearing System

Dual bearings per wheel hold the axle on a true straight line. Less wobble, less lateral deviation, more of the CO₂ thrust converting into actual forward motion.

21.4 % of total drag
Innov · 03

Aerofins + Halo Lip

Small fins above the halo and a vertical lip below it. Dirty incoming air becomes straightened streamlines that get pushed cleanly over and around the cockpit.

Inspired · Modern F1
Innov · 04

Mathematical Race Model

900+ lines of Python integrating thrust curves, wheel friction, drag, tether yaw and guide-base height. Predicts race time, side displacement and yaw before a chip is cut.

3,000 Monte-Carlo runs

Race Log · From Delhi to Sentosa

A garage, three Mks,
seventeen sponsors.

SUPERCHARGED started on a single workbench at The British School, New Delhi. Three seasons later we represented India at the Aramco STEM Racing World Finals in Singapore. Below: every milestone that mattered.

World Finals · Singapore · 09·25
  1. 04 · 2024

    Garage Year Zero

    First prototype CAD on Fusion 360. The team commits to planting one tree for every car prototype manufactured between April 2024 and October 2025.

    Mk 0 · concept
  2. 11 · 2024

    F4 India Promotion · Coimbatore

    Met India's top motorsport professionals. Turned a race weekend into a brand-awareness drive for STEM Racing across India.

    + 500 student reach
  3. Q1 · 2025

    Regional Finals · The British School

    Volunteered as mentors to other teams at the regionals hosted at our school. Confirmed Mk II and the path to Nationals.

    Mentorship · India hub
  4. Q2 · 2025

    National Finals · World Finals Selection

    Booked the Singapore ticket as Team T62 representing India at the Aramco STEM Racing World Finals 2025.

    T62·India
  5. 08 · 2025

    Project Kick-off · 03 August

    Final two-month sprint into Singapore. Six portfolios, two cars, one halo, one helmet, one pit display, one ten-minute presentation.

    Mk III · final iteration
  6. 25 · 09 · 2025 → 03 · 10 · 2025

    Aramco STEM Racing World Finals · Singapore

    Resorts World Sentosa. Three days of judging, scrutineering, knockouts. Best test time before parc fermé: 1.330 seconds. Goal: be the first Indian team to win.
